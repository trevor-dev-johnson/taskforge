## What TaskForge Does

1. Provides a backend service for managing tasks within teams.

2. Organizes tasks under projects to establish clear ownership and scope.

3. Enforces role-based access for users (admins and members).

4. Manages task state transitions using a fixed, predefined workflow.

5. Runs a background job to identify and process overdue tasks.

## Who Uses It?

TaskForge is used by teams within an organization, including engineers, managers, and admins, who need a simple way to track tasks across projects with clear ownership and role-based access.

## What Does TaskForge Not Do?

1. Provide a user interface or frontend application (API-only service).

2. Support real-time collaboration or notifications.

3. Handle file uploads, attachments, or comments.

4. Support custom workflows or user-defined task states.

5. Aim to replace full-featured project management tools (e.g., Jira or Asana).

6. Include multi-tenant billing, subscriptions, or payments.

7. Optimize for massive scale or high availability beyond typical internal use.
